Overview

This is a website about Treaty 5, concerning the First Nations. This website will describe some of the events, locations, and details that had occurred within Treaty 5.

Locations

Treaty 5 was negotiated at Berens River and Norway House in 1875.

Treaty 5 mainly consists of the mid-south area of what now is Manitoba, reaching a bit into Saskatchewan and Ontario.

Parties & Promises

The involved nations were:

  • Swampy Cree
  • Ojibway (Saulteaux)
  • Dene

The promises consisted of:

  1. Everyone gets 160 acres of land.
  2. Everyone gets $5 annually.
  3. Headmen get $15 annually.
  4. Chiefs get $25 annually.
  5. Everyone keeps the right to continue hunting and fishing outside of locations where there are settlements, mines, or lumbering.
  6. They will be provided with tools once.
  7. Schools and education will be maintained.
  8. $500 worth of ammunition and twine will be given.
  9. $500 will be given to the people to start off with.

Modern Implementation

Treaty promises are mostly still kept intact today. One example is qualified personel being granted $5 annually, in favor of the Treaty.

Urban reserves such as Chemawawin or Wàwàtéwàk Corridor have been created to allow First Nations the opportunities to build community.
