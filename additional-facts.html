Additional Facts

Territory Coverage

When Treaty #5 was signed, the First Nations were assigned approximately 100,000 square miles or 160,934.4 square kilometers. This spanned from the majority of Manitoba to small portions of Saskatchewan.
However, after the adhesion was signed, it grew to approximately 133,400 square miles or 214,686.49 kilometers. This spanned from Ontario to larger portions of Saskatchewan, on top of the territory that was already covered by the original Treaty #5.

Adhesion

Treaty #5 was not truly finalized until its adhesion in 1910. The adhesion was a set of additions to the original treaty, additions with the goal of completing the treaty that wasn't entirely done for a long time; 35 years to be exact.

The image in the previous section contains the massive amount of territory provided in this adhesion. The areas in white are the Treaty #5 adhesion territorial additions. The 1908 adhesion involved Split Lake and Nelson House Cree, and the 1910 adhesion involved Sandy Lake communities, later leading to the creation of the Deer Lake band.
